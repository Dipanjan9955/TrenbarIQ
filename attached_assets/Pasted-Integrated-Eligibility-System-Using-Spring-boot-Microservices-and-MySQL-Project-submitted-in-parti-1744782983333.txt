Abstract
The Rhode Island Integrated Eligibility System (RI-IES) is a comprehensive
application designed to streamline the process of providing health and insurance plans
for citizens in need within the state of Rhode Island. The application offers various
plans such as SNAP, CCAP, Medicaid, Medicare, and RIW, each tailored to specific
eligibility criteria and demographic groups. Through a series of modules, including
administration, user management, application registration, data collection, eligibility
determination, correspondence, benefit issuance, and reporting, RI-IES efficiently
manages the entire lifecycle of plan applications and approvals.
The system operates by collecting and analyzing citizen data to determine eligibility
for the applied plans. Approved citizens receive benefits on a monthly basis, while
those whose data does not meet eligibility criteria are denied access to the plans. The
RI-IES application is exclusively for Rhode Island State citizens, ensuring targeted
assistance to those in need within the state.
Key features include an administrative module for managing plans and case workers,
a user module for registration, login, and dashboard access, as well as specialized
modules for application registration, data collection, eligibility determination,
correspondence management, benefit issuance, and reporting. Reports generated by
RI-IES include plan-wise, status-wise, and monthly reports in Excel and PDF formats,
providing comprehensive insights into program performance and citizen assistance
metrics.
Overall, RI-IES represents a vital tool in the efficient and equitable distribution of health
and insurance plans to Rhode Island State citizens, facilitating access to essential
services for those in need while ensuring accountability and transparency in program
administration.

CHAPTER 1
INTRODUCTION
1.1 OVERVIEW:
The RI-IES (Integrated Eligibility System) is a sophisticated application designed
to address the health and insurance needs of Rhode Island State citizens who are
facing financial constraints. The system encompasses a range of plans, including SNAP,
CCAP, Medicaid, Medicare, and RIW, each customized to specific eligibility conditions.
Through the application of cutting-edge Spring Boot microservices, the RI-IES aims to
streamline the plan application process, determine citizen eligibility, and facilitate the
issuance of benefits.
1.2 PROJECT DESCRIPTION:
The RI-IES is a comprehensive application developed to provide health and
insurance plans for Rhode Island State citizens in need. The application offers the
following plans on behalf of the government:
1 SNAP: Supplement Nutrition Assistance Program (Food Stamp Program)
2 CCAP: Child Care Assistance Program (Child Care Program)
3 Medicaid: Health Plan
4 Medicare: Health Plan for senior citizens (aged 65 and above)
5 RIW: Unemployment Plan (Rhode Island Works)
Each plan has specific eligibility criteria. Citizens in need can apply for these plans
through the RI-IES application. Based on the eligibility rules of each plan and the data
provided by the citizens, the RI-IES system determines the eligibility of citizens for the
applied plans.
â€¢ Approved citizens receive benefits from the state government on a monthly
basis.
â€¢ If a citizen's data matches the eligibility criteria of a plan, they are approved for
the plan.
â€¢ If a citizen's data does not meet the eligibility criteria of a plan, they are denied
access to the plan.

Note: The plans offered by the RI-IES application are exclusively for Rhode Island
State Citizens.
Modules of IES:
1.1 Admin: Manages plans and case workers within the application.
1.2 User: Handles registration, login, forgot password, and provides a dashboard for
users.
1.3 AR (Application Registration): Allows citizens to create applications for plans.
1.4 DC (Data Collection): Gathers necessary information for citizen plans.
1.5 ED (Eligibility Determination): Verifies citizen eligibility for the plans.
1.6 CO (Correspondence): Sends notices and communications to citizens.
1.7 BI (Benefit Issuance): Distributes benefit amounts to approved citizens.
1.8 Reports: Generates various reports (in Excel and PDF formats) including plan-wise,
status-wise, and monthly reports.
1.3 PURPOSE:
The primary purpose of the RI-IES is to provide a centralized and efficient
platform for managing health and insurance plans for citizens with financial
constraints. By automating the application process, eligibility determination, and
benefit issuance, the system aims to reduce administrative overhead and ensure that
eligible citizens receive timely support from the Rhode Island State Government.
1.4 PROJECT SCOPE:
The scope of the project is defined by the targeted plans, namely SNAP, CCAP,
Medicaid, Medicare, and RIW, catering specifically to Rhode Island State citizens. The
system encompasses multiple modules, including administration, user management,
application registration, data collection, eligibility determination, correspondence,
benefit issuance, and reporting. The project's scope extends to the development of a
user-friendly interface, robust backend microservices, and seamless integration with
external services.

3.2 SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE:
In the Integrated Eligibility System (IES) project, we have adopted a microservices
architecture to structure the system, utilizing the Spring Boot framework for
development. This architectural approach offers several advantages that align with the
project's requirements and objectives:
â€¢ Modularity: Microservices architecture decomposes the system into smaller,
independent services, each responsible for specific business functions or
features. This modularity allows for better organization of code and enables
teams to work on individual services autonomously, promoting faster
development cycles and easier maintenance.
8 | P a g e
â€¢ Scalability: With microservices, each service can be scaled independently based
on its workload and resource requirements. This scalability ensures that the
system can handle varying levels of traffic and workload efficiently, improving
performance and responsiveness.
â€¢ Flexibility: Microservices architecture promotes flexibility and agility in system
design and development. New features or changes can be implemented and
deployed independently in specific services without affecting the entire system.
This decoupling of services minimizes the risk of system-wide failures and
allows for faster iteration and innovation.
â€¢ Resilience: By distributing functionality across multiple services, microservices
architecture enhances system resilience and fault tolerance. If one service
encounters issues or failures, it does not impact the entire system, as other
services can continue to function independently. Additionally, redundant
instances of critical services can be deployed to mitigate single points of failure.
â€¢ Technology Diversity: Microservices architecture enables the use of different
technologies and programming languages for individual services based on their
specific requirements. This flexibility allows teams to select the most
appropriate tools and technologies for each service, optimizing performance,
and productivity.
â€¢ Deployment and Updates: Microservices architecture facilitates continuous
deployment and updates by breaking down the system into smaller
components. Each service can be deployed independently, enabling faster
release cycles and reducing the risk associated with deploying large, monolithic
applications.
â€¢ System Expansion: As the Integrated Eligibility System evolves and grows,
microservices architecture supports seamless system expansion. New services
can be added or existing services can be modified or replaced without
disrupting the entire system, enabling scalability and future-proofing the
architecture.
By adopting a microservices architecture in conjunction with the Spring Boot
framework, the Integrated Eligibility System is well-equipped to meet the project's
objectives of scalability, flexibility, resilience, and maintainability, while facilitating
efficient development, deployment, and system expansion.

4.4 NON-FUNCTIONALITIES & REQUIREMENTS:
There are a lot of software requirements which are included in the nonfunctional
requirements in this project, which includes Security, Performance,
Usability, Scalability. Availability, Reliability. Compatibility and Accessibility.
1. Security: The portal should ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and
availability of user data, as well as protect against unauthorized access or
modification.
2. Performance: The portal should be able to handle a large number of
concurrent users and requests, while maintaining a fast response time.
3. Usability: The portal should be easy to use, navigate, and understand for both
students and teachers, with clear instructions and feedback.
4. Scalability: The portal should be able to scale up or down based on changing
user needs, without compromising performance or functionality.
5. Availability: The portal should be available 24/7, with minimal downtime for
maintenance or upgrades.
6. Reliability: The portal should be reliable and free from errors or bugs, with
automated testing and monitoring to detect and fix issues.
7. Compatibility: The portal should be compatible with a wide range of browsers,
devices, and operating systems, to ensure that users can access it from
anywhere and on any device.
8. Accessibility: The portal should be accessible to users with disabilities, with
features such as text-to-speech and keyboard navigation.
These non-functional requirements are important for ensuring the success and
usability of the Assignment Portal, and should be considered throughout the
development process
